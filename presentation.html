📊 Results Interpretation

What do these metrics mean for real patients?

Sensitivity (Recall) — "catch rate"

Of all patients who truly have the disease, what percentage did our model correctly identify?

Example: A Sensitivity of 85% means we catch 85 out of 100 truly sick patients. The remaining 15 are missed — false negatives.

Clinical Impact of Low Sensitivity

In healthcare, missing a sick patient (false negative) can be life-threatening. A model with 60% sensitivity misses 4 in 10 patients — unacceptable for screening tools.

This is why we prioritize Sensitivity over Accuracy in clinical ML.

⚖️ Bias & Fairness Audit

Subgroup analysis — gender & age bands

How It Works

  • Model is evaluated separately for each subgroup (Male/Female, Age bands)
  • Sensitivity per subgroup is compared to overall sensitivity
  • Alert triggered when any gap exceeds 10 percentage points
  • Age bands: raw values use fixed ranges; normalized values use quantile-based tertiles

⚠️ Example disparity: If overall sensitivity = 82% but Female subgroup = 68%, the gap is 14 pp → BIAS ALERT

🧠 Key Technical Decisions

🔬

Real scikit-learn

Actual model training — not simulated. Metrics vary with data and hyperparameters.

🐳

3-Container Split

Frontend, Backend API, and ML Service run independently. Clean separation of concerns.

🔍

Ablation Explainability

Per-patient waterfall — works across all 6 models without SHAP/LIME dependency.

⚖️

SMOTE Train-Only

Synthetic rows tagged with sentinel column, excluded from test split. No data leakage.

📏

10pp Bias Threshold

Calibrated to avoid noise from small datasets while catching clinically meaningful gaps.

🔒

Step Gating

Enforced prerequisite completion mirrors real clinical ML workflow constraints.

📚 What We Learned

Technical lessons from building HealthML

🧪 Test early with real data

SMOTE leakage and Unicode PDF bugs were invisible with synthetic data. Real CSV uploads exposed them immediately.

🏗️ Microservices add complexity

Cross-container networking and environment variables cost significant debugging time. Worth it for clean architecture, but plan for the overhead.

⚖️ Fairness isn't an afterthought

Retrofitting bias analysis in Sprint 4 was expensive. Building fairness hooks from Sprint 2 would have been significantly cheaper.

📝 Plain language matters

Clinicians ignored metrics until labels were rephrased. "% of truly positive patients caught" works better than "Recall".

🚪 Gate reviews prevent debt

Enforcing sprint gate criteria (live demo + passing tests) prevented unfinished work from accumulating.

📈 Velocity improved over time

Sprint 1: 3 story points → Sprint 5: 11 story points. Weekly instructor feedback accelerated decisions and reduced rework.
